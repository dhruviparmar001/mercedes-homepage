Karl benz

Karl Benz was a German engineer who invented the first practical automobile powered by an internal combustion engine, laying the foundation for the modern car industry.

he developed a deep interest in mechanics and engineering from an early age. In 1885, Benz built the Benz Patent-Motorwagen, which is widely considered the worldâ€™s first true car. It was a three-wheeled vehicle powered by a gasoline engine, and it marked the beginning of the modern automotive industry. His wife, Bertha Benz, played a crucial role in promoting the invention by undertaking the first long-distance journey in the car, proving its reliability. Karl Benz later merged his company with Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft, forming what would become Mercedes-Benz. His work laid the foundation for personal transportation and transformed global society.
